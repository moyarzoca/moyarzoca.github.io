Based on [New BPS solitons in N = 4 gauged supergravity and black holes in Einstein-Yang-Mills-dilaton theory](/publication/2021-new-bps-solitons-in-N4-gauged-supergravity)

Abstract: I will discuss novel results regarding the problem of finding BPS solutions in N=4 SU(2)xSU(2) gauged supergravity in four dimensions. We construct a new supersymmetric solution in the Abelian sector of the theory which describes a 1/4 BPS soliton. Also in the Abelian sector, but now for a spherically symmetric ansatz we find a new solution whose supersymmetric nature was overlooked in the previous literature. We also construct solutions in the non-Abelian sector of the theory by considering the meron ansatz for SU(2). We identify these configurations, including the planar case, as a new family of black holes and solitons that lead to the exact integration of scalar probes, even in the presence of a non-minimal coupling with the Ricci scalar. We find that the equation for the radial dependence of the scalar probe can be integrated in terms of hypergeometric functions leading to an exact expression for the frequencies. We find both purely damped modes and exponentially growing unstable modes depending on the values of the non-minimal coupling parameter, for both black holes and solitons. Comments regarding the embedding of theses new solution in type IA supergravity in 10D are also given.
